Paper Summary Skill

This skill summarizes academic papers into structured research notes.

It is designed for social science and demography research workflows.

Use this skill when the user asks to:

summarize a research paper
extract structure from a paper
make a literature review note
analyze a journal article
prepare notes for a thesis or literature review


Output Structure

Always produce the following sections:


1 Research Question
What question does the paper try to answer?


2 Theoretical Framework
What theory or conceptual framework does the paper rely on?


3 Data
Dataset used
Country / region
Sample size
Time period


4 Methods
Statistical or analytical methods used.

Examples:
regression
event history analysis
difference-in-differences
qualitative analysis

Explain briefly why the method fits the research question.


5 Main Findings
Key empirical results.

Prefer bullet points.


6 Limitations
What the paper cannot answer or possible weaknesses.


7 Relevance to My Research
Explain how the paper relates to:

migration
demography
gender
social inequality
the user's thesis

If possible, suggest how it could be cited in a literature review.


Evidence Rule
When possible, base the summary on the original text of the paper.

Indicate the section of the paper when relevant 
(e.g., Introduction, Data, Methods, Results).


Language Requirement
Output must be written in Chinese unless the user explicitly requests another language.


Style Rules
Academic tone but concise.
Prefer structured bullet points.
Avoid unnecessary verbosity.


Goal
The goal is to transform papers into reusable literature review notes.
