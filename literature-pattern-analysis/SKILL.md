# Literature Pattern Analysis

This skill analyzes a structured literature matrix produced by the paper-matrix-extract skill.

The goal is to identify patterns across studies and help build a literature review.

The input should be a table (TSV, CSV, or Excel-like format) where each row represents a paper and columns follow the literature matrix schema.

# Analysis Tasks

The skill must analyze the literature matrix and produce structured insights across the following dimensions:

1. Dominant research topics
2. Most common outcome types
3. Most frequently used theories
4. Most common independent variables
5. Most frequent control variables
6. Most common mechanisms
7. Most used research methods
8. Most used model specifications
9. Identification strategies used in the literature
10. Data sources and data types
11. Level of analysis patterns
12. Gender focus distribution
13. Country or regional focus patterns
14. Frequently cited theoretical references
15. Recurrent research gaps
16. Clusters of closely related studies
17. Under-studied combinations (e.g. gender × migration intention)

---

# Pattern Extraction Rules

When analyzing patterns:

• Count frequencies of repeated items  
• Identify dominant approaches  
• Identify minority approaches  
• Identify missing combinations  
• Identify methodological concentration  

Focus on **repetition and absence**.

Do not summarize papers individually.

Instead synthesize across the entire dataset.

---

# Variable Frequency Analysis

For columns that contain lists separated by semicolons:

Theory  
Main IV  
Controls  
Mechanism  
Key Theory References  

Split the items and count frequencies.

Report the most common variables.

Example:

Most common control variables:
age (18 papers)
education (16 papers)
gender (15 papers)

---

# Method Landscape

Identify the dominant methodological approaches.

Report:

Most common Method Type  
Most common Model Specification  
Presence of causal identification strategies

Example output:

Method landscape:
Regression models dominate (24 papers)
Logit / multinomial logit most common
Few causal identification strategies

---

# Theory Landscape

Identify theoretical clusters.

Group similar theories when possible.

Example:

Migration transition theory  
Aspiration–ability framework  
Relative deprivation theory

Explain which frameworks dominate the field.

---

# Data Landscape

Analyze:

Data Source  
Data Type  
Level of Analysis

Example:

Most studies rely on cross-sectional surveys.  
Few use panel data.  
Most analyses are at the individual level.

---

# Gender and Population Focus

Analyze:

Gender Focus  
Country/Region

Example:

Most studies analyze mixed samples.  
Few focus specifically on women.

---

# Research Gap Detection

Analyze the Research Gap column and identify recurring themes.

Examples:

lack of gender perspective  
lack of irregular migration analysis  
lack of micro-level evidence

Then identify **structural gaps**:

Example:

Few studies examine:
education × gender × migration intention
